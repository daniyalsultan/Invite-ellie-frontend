Meeting Impact Score - Calculation Parameters
===============================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The Impact Score is a 0-100 rating that measures the overall effectiveness and productivity of a meeting. It's calculated by analyzing the meeting transcript, summary, and action items using Groq AI (Llama 3.3 70B model).

SCORING BREAKDOWN (Total: 100 points)
--------------------------------------
The impact score is calculated based on 4 key dimensions, each worth 25 points:

1. DECISION-MAKING EFFECTIVENESS (0-25 points)
------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
- How many concrete decisions were made during the meeting
- Clarity of decisions (specific vs. vague)
- Whether decisions have clear outcomes and next steps
- Resolution of conflicts or disagreements
- Consensus among participants

High Score Indicators:
- Multiple clear decisions made
- Decisions are specific and actionable
- All participants agree on outcomes
- Decisions have clear timelines and owners

Low Score Indicators:
- No decisions made
- Vague or ambiguous decisions
- Decisions deferred to "later"
- Conflicts unresolved

Example Scoring:
- 20-25 points: Multiple clear decisions with owners and timelines
- 15-19 points: Some decisions made but missing details
- 10-14 points: Few decisions, mostly vague
- 0-9 points: No concrete decisions made


2. ACTION ITEM CLARITY AND OWNERSHIP (0-25 points)
---------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
- Number of action items identified
- Clarity of action items (specific vs. vague)
- Whether each action item has a clear owner assigned
- Whether action items have deadlines or timelines
- Actionability of items (can they be executed?)

High Score Indicators:
- Multiple action items with clear owners
- Each action item has a deadline
- Action items are specific and measurable
- Clear ownership assigned to each task

Low Score Indicators:
- No action items identified
- Action items without owners
- Vague action items ("we'll figure it out later")
- No deadlines or timelines

Example Scoring:
- 20-25 points: 3+ action items, all with owners and deadlines
- 15-19 points: Some action items, but missing owners or deadlines
- 10-14 points: Few action items, mostly vague
- 0-9 points: No clear action items or all without owners


3. STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT AND COVERAGE (0-25 points)
-----------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
- Whether all relevant stakeholders are present
- Participation level of attendees
- Whether key decision-makers are involved
- Coverage of all topics that need input
- Balance of participation (not dominated by one person)

High Score Indicators:
- All relevant stakeholders present
- Balanced participation from all attendees
- Key decision-makers involved in discussions
- All topics covered with proper input

Low Score Indicators:
- Missing key stakeholders
- One person dominates the conversation
- Important topics discussed without relevant people
- Low participation from some attendees

Example Scoring:
- 20-25 points: All stakeholders present, balanced participation
- 15-19 points: Most stakeholders present, good participation
- 10-14 points: Some stakeholders missing, uneven participation
- 0-9 points: Key stakeholders missing, poor engagement


4. MEETING PRODUCTIVITY AND FOLLOW-THROUGH POTENTIAL (0-25 points)
------------------------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
- Meeting structure and organization
- Time efficiency (staying on topic)
- Whether meeting ended with clear next steps
- Follow-up plans and accountability
- Overall meeting flow and productivity

High Score Indicators:
- Well-structured meeting with clear agenda
- Stayed on topic, efficient use of time
- Clear recap and next steps at the end
- Follow-up meetings or check-ins scheduled
- High information-to-time ratio

Low Score Indicators:
- No clear agenda or structure
- Went off-topic frequently
- Meeting ended without recap
- No follow-up plans
- Low productivity (little accomplished)

Example Scoring:
- 20-25 points: Highly productive, clear structure, good follow-through
- 15-19 points: Productive but could be more structured
- 10-14 points: Some productivity, but disorganized
- 0-9 points: Low productivity, no clear outcomes


HOW IT'S CALCULATED
-------------------

Input Data Analyzed:
1. Meeting Transcript (first 6000 characters)
   - Full conversation text
   - Speaker identification
   - Timestamps

2. Meeting Summary (if available)
   - AI-generated summary
   - Key discussion points

3. Action Items (if available)
   - List of action items extracted
   - Owners and deadlines (if mentioned)

4. Previous Meeting Context (last 5 meetings)
   - Summaries from previous meetings
   - Historical context for comparison

AI Analysis Process:
1. Groq AI (Llama 3.3 70B) analyzes the transcript
2. Evaluates each dimension (0-25 points each)
3. Calculates total score (sum of 4 dimensions = 0-100)
4. Provides breakdown showing score for each dimension

Output Format:
{
  "impact_score": 75.5,  // Total score (0-100)
  "impact_breakdown": {
    "decision_making": 20,           // 0-25 points
    "action_clarity": 18,           // 0-25 points
    "stakeholder_engagement": 19,   // 0-25 points
    "productivity": 18.5            // 0-25 points
  }
}


SCORE INTERPRETATION
--------------------

90-100: Excellent Meeting
- Highly effective decision-making
- Clear action items with owners
- All stakeholders engaged
- Very productive and well-structured

75-89: Good Meeting
- Effective decisions made
- Most action items have owners
- Good stakeholder coverage
- Productive with minor improvements needed

50-74: Average Meeting
- Some decisions made but missing details
- Action items present but unclear ownership
- Some stakeholders missing or disengaged
- Moderate productivity

25-49: Below Average Meeting
- Few or vague decisions
- Action items without clear owners
- Missing key stakeholders
- Low productivity

0-24: Poor Meeting
- No concrete decisions
- No clear action items
- Critical stakeholders missing
- Very low productivity


FACTORS THAT AFFECT EACH DIMENSION
-----------------------------------

Decision-Making Effectiveness:
+ Number of decisions made
+ Specificity of decisions
+ Decision ownership
+ Conflict resolution
- Vague commitments
- Deferred decisions

Action Item Clarity:
+ Number of action items
+ Owner assignment
+ Deadline specification
+ Action item specificity
- Vague action items
- Missing owners

Stakeholder Engagement:
+ All relevant people present
+ Balanced participation
+ Key decision-makers involved
+ Topic coverage
- Missing stakeholders
- Uneven participation

Meeting Productivity:
+ Clear agenda
+ Time efficiency
+ Meeting recap
+ Follow-up plans
- No structure
- Off-topic discussions


EXAMPLE SCENARIOS
-----------------

High Impact Meeting (Score: 85)
- Decision-Making: 22/25 - Made 5 clear decisions with owners
- Action Clarity: 21/25 - 6 action items, all with owners and deadlines
- Stakeholder Engagement: 21/25 - All stakeholders present, balanced participation
- Productivity: 21/25 - Well-structured, ended with clear recap

Low Impact Meeting (Score: 35)
- Decision-Making: 8/25 - No concrete decisions, everything deferred
- Action Clarity: 9/25 - 2 vague action items, no owners
- Stakeholder Engagement: 10/25 - Missing key stakeholders
- Productivity: 8/25 - No structure, ended without recap
